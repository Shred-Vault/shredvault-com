The problem

Every sensitive document you've set aside is still out there.

Tax returns. Bank statements. Medical records. Pay stubs. Patient files. Employee contracts. The gap between generating these documents and disposing of them securely is where trust breaks down.

01

Identity at risk

146 million Americans have had sensitive personal information compromised. Most breaches don't start with a hack. They start with a document in the wrong bin.

02

Compliance liability

Remote work created a gap most organizations haven't formally addressed. Your employees handle sensitive documents at home. You are responsible for what happens to them.

03

A missed opportunity

Paper is recyclable. Most of it still goes to landfill. There's no infrastructure connecting secure disposal to the circular economy — until now.

The Trust Platform

A promise to behave, protect, and pay attention to the future.

ShredVault is not a shredding service. It is a platform — and a platform is only as trustworthy as the promise beneath it. Ours has three parts.

Behave — The Two-Step Rule

Every primary action in two steps or fewer. From "I want to deal with this pile" to "the bag is on its way" in under 60 seconds. Consistency is behavior. Behavior is trust.

Protect — Certificate of Destruction

Every deposit generates a verifiable Certificate of Destruction — issued per transaction, stored in your compliance dashboard, ready for audit. Not a receipt. A record.

Pay attention to the future — 100% recycled

Every bag — the bag itself, and everything inside it — is shredded and recycled. No competitor makes that claim. For organizations with ESG commitments, this is auditable impact.

ShredVault Platform Thesis

The Coinstar Analogy.

Coinstar turned coin redemption — an annoying, low-trust transaction — into a familiar, self-serve retail moment. You walk up, you trust it, you leave with a receipt.

ShredVault is doing the same thing for document destruction. The kiosk is in a location you already visit. The deposit takes two steps. You leave with confirmation. Trust is built before you even think to question it.

That's not a feature. That's the promise made operational.
